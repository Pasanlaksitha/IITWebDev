The Origins of Project Gaia

How we started, How we do it


Project Gaia: A Sanctuary for All Wildlife in Yala National Park Nestled within the lush wilderness of Yala National Park, Sri Lanka, Project Gaia stands as a beacon of hope for wildlife in need. Aligned with the UN sustainability goal of "Life on Land," our vision is bold and clear: to become a sanctuary for all creatures that call Yala home. At the heart of Project Gaia lies our wildlife veterinary clinic. This specialized facility provides a lifeline for injured and vulnerable animals. Here, our dedicated team of veterinarians and veterinary technicians treat a wide range of patients – from majestic elephants caught in human-wildlife conflict to the park's diverse avian population. Our interventions range from emergency wound care and nutritional support to critical surgeries and rehabilitation programs. Human-elephant conflict is a significant threat to both humans and these gentle giants. When such encounters occur, our team takes swift action. We tranquilize and relocate elephants who have strayed into human settlements, minimizing harm to both parties. Back at the clinic, any injuries sustained by the elephants during the conflict are treated, ensuring their safe return to their herds. Our commitment to wildlife extends beyond the walls of the clinic. We understand that a healthy environment is vital for a thriving animal population. This is why Project Gaia actively participates in beach cleanups along the stunning coastline bordering Yala National Park. Plastic pollution poses a significant threat to marine life, particularly to the park's sea turtles. By removing plastic debris from nesting sites and educating local communities on responsible waste disposal, we strive to create safe havens for these magnificent creatures.


Project Gaia fosters collaboration and partnerships. We work closely with the Department of Wildlife Conservation (DWC) to ensure our efforts align with national conservation strategies. Additionally, we actively engage with local communities, fostering a sense of shared responsibility for Yala's wildlife. Through educational workshops and awareness programs, we aim to bridge the gap between humans and animals, promoting peaceful coexistence. Project Gaia is more than just a wildlife rescue and rehabilitation center. We are a testament to the power of human compassion and dedication. Every animal treated, every beach cleaned, and every community member educated brings us closer to our vision of a sanctuary for all wildlife in Yala National Park. Here's a glimpse into the potential impact of Project Gaia:


Reduced wildlife mortality: With a dedicated veterinary clinic and swift intervention during human-wildlife conflicts, the project can significantly reduce animal deaths due to injuries and illnesses. Improved animal welfare: Through comprehensive medical care and rehabilitation programs, Project Gaia can ensure the well-being of injured and orphaned animals, increasing their chances of survival and successful reintroduction into the wild. Enhanced habitat protection: Beach cleanups and community awareness programs can contribute to a cleaner and safer environment for marine wildlife, particularly nesting sea turtles. Project Gaia is a story of hope, resilience, and the unwavering belief in the interconnectedness of all living things. By supporting our project, you become a vital partner in safeguarding the rich tapestry of life within Yala National Park and contributing to a more sustainable future for our planet..
